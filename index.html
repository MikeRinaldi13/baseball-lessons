I started at the University of Tampa, where I redshirted and spent a year doing nothing but learning. Kevin Kiermaier, Platinum Glove outfielder for the Tampa Bay Rays, loved to come out and play and practice at our field. Having an established MLB player around like that, you soak up everything you can. I transferred to Seminole State, where former MLB players like Adam Haseley, outfielder for the Philadelphia Phillies, would come in and work with us. Then I got seriously hurt. Labrum surgery the fall before the season. I couldn't swing a bat, so I did what I always did: watched, listened, and learned. When I finally got healthy and got my shot at Greensboro College, it all came together. I hit .358 with 8 home runs, 34 RBI, 11 doubles, a triple, and 12 walks in 34 games, posting a .421 on-base percentage and .659 slugging percentage. The system works. I know it works because I lived it. And honestly, I wish I had this knowledge when I was 12 years old. That's why I do this. To give the next generation a real foundation so they're not figuring it out the hard way like I did.

The Approach

Violence through the middle. Every part of the swing has one job: direct energy back to the pitcher. Everything else is built around making that happen.

0
Foundation
Spine Angle
Everything starts with getting into the right position and holding it through contact. A consistent spine angle keeps your swing working linearly, so your energy stays directed back to the pitcher instead of leaking out to the side.
1
Lower Half
The Engine — Ground Up
Power comes from the ground. Back foot loads, front foot plants. The legs drive everything.
2
Rotation
Fire, Then Hit the Brakes
Start the rotation, fire hard, then hit the brakes fast. That sudden stop is what releases the hands and creates real bat speed.
3
Hands
Tight and Whip
Hands tight to the body, then extend through the pitch. No casting. No looping.
4
Hitting Zone
Short to It, Long Through It
Take the shortest path to the ball, then stay through the pitch as long as possible. Quick in, violent through.
⚡

Violence Through The Middle

Direction back to the pitcher. Every cue, every correction, every session is built around driving the barrel through the middle of the field with intent.
